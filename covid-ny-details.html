COVID-19 in New York State - Demographic Analysis of COVID-19 Outcomes

This project analyzes the impact of demographic and socio-economic factors on COVID-19 infection, death, and fatality rates across four pandemic waves in New York State.

Overview

The goal of this analysis is to understand the role of demographics in the spread and impact of COVID-19. Using data from multiple sources, the project examines key predictors like age, ethnicity, and poverty to identify patterns across counties in New York State.

Methodology

  • Data Collection: Integrated datasets from Syracuse.com and the American Community Survey.
  • Feature Engineering: Calculated infection, death, and fatality rates for each wave.
  • Clustering: Applied K-means clustering to segment counties based on COVID-19 outcomes.
  • Modeling: Evaluated Linear Regression, Random Forest, and SVM models for predictive analysis.
  • Visualization: Created heatmaps and maps to explore geographic and demographic trends.

Key Findings

  • Age and diabetes were significant predictors of COVID-19 outcomes.
  • Linear Regression performed best among the models, but highlighted the need for more data.
  • Socioeconomic factors, like poverty and education, influenced infection and fatality rates.

Visualizations

Technologies Used

Tools: Python, Tableau, Matplotlib, Seaborn
Libraries: Pandas, Scikit-learn, PCA Analysis
Data Integration: Combined demographic and geographic data from multiple sources.

Challenges and Limitations

  • Small sample size (64 counties) limited statistical significance.
  • Potential inaccuracies due to merging diverse datasets.

Conclusion

The analysis demonstrated the significant impact of demographic factors on COVID-19 outcomes. While linear regression provided the best performance, the insights underscore the need for larger, more granular datasets for more robust conclusions.
